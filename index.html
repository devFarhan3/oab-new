Internet Banking

Oman Arab Bank has a state-of-the-art online banking platform – OAB Online, for both internet and mobile banking.

You can sign up to OAB Online banking and perform day-to-day banking transactions, submit service requests, make inquiries, and much more, in a safe and secure manner, 24 hours a day, 7 days per week.

Automated Teller Machines

Enjoy 24-hours access to your account through our extensive network of over 150 ATMs across Oman. Our ATMs come with a range of services including:

  • Cash withdrawal
  • Cash deposit
  • Balance enquiry
  • Change withdrawal limit
  • Utility bill payments
  • Money transfer between personal and other OAB accounts
  • Credit card payment
  • Donations
  • Update GSM
  • Account Services

Fil-Khidma

Connect with our ‘Fil-Khidma’ call centre for any banking related questions, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

You can choose self-service banking to access your accounts and cards information or talk to one of our highly knowledgeable service representatives to avail OAB services on the go including:

  • Account balance enquiry
  • Utility bill payments
  • Balance enquiry
  • Credit cards payments
  • Money transfer between personal and other OAB accounts
  • Account management requests: including debit card and cheque book related services
  • Mobile top-ups
  • Transaction and credit limit management

Tawasul

Free-of-charge SMS alerts for all your banking transactions provide you complete sense of security.

Sign up to Tawasul for on-demand inquiries and financial services including:

  • Balance enquiry
  • Transaction history
  • Balance enquiry
  • Utility bill payments
  • Money transfer between own accounts

International Remittances – iSend

Introducing iSend – our new easy-to-use and instant international remittance service via the OAB Online App.

iSend allows you to transfer money at competitive rates to friends and family internationally from the convenience of your own home.

Currently, the transfer service is available to India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Singapore, UAE, and the EU SEPA (countries that use Euro as their currency). Phase two will see the extension of the service to the Philippines, Egypt, Morocco, the United Kingdom, and the United States of America.
